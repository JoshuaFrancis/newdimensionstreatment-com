Programme overview

Three treatment levels built for lasting recovery.

01

Residential treatment

A structured live-in treatment environment providing thirty, sixty, and ninety-day residential programmes for individuals requiring removal from their current environment to achieve early recovery stability. Daily programming includes individual therapy, group therapy, psychoeducation, 12-step and SMART Recovery facilitation, family involvement sessions, and evidence-based addiction medicine where clinically indicated. Residential programmes are staffed by addiction counsellors, registered clinical counsellors, and physicians specialising in addiction medicine.

02

Dual diagnosis treatment

Integrated treatment for clients with co-occurring substance use disorders and mental health conditions including depression, anxiety disorders, PTSD, bipolar disorder, and personality disorders. Effective dual diagnosis treatment addresses both conditions simultaneously rather than sequentially, which substantially improves outcomes compared to treating addiction and mental health separately. The programme uses a unified treatment model where the same clinical team manages both the addiction and psychiatric components of care.

03

Aftercare and continuing care

Structured continuing care planning, sober living referrals, and long-term recovery coaching that bridges the gap between residential treatment completion and independent sustainable sobriety. Research consistently shows that treatment outcomes are determined less by what happens during residential care and more by the quality and continuity of support in the twelve to twenty-four months following discharge. Aftercare programmes include regular check-ins, peer support networks, family education, and rapid re-access to higher levels of care during relapse.
